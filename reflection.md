What are your initial impressions of how `Unit Testing` affects your programming?
What worked, what didn't, what advice would you give someone taking this course in the future?


My initial impression of unit testing is that it forces you to think much more carefully about what your code is supposed to do, not just what it does. Before this assignment I would just guess the output, but writing formal assumptions made me realize how easy a bug can just go unnoticed. For example, the constructor in Computer.java looked completely normal but there were a ton of things that were wrong with the setting of parameters.
Getting set up was honestly the hardest part. I had issues with VSCode for a while. The JUnit configuration wasn't working and I kept running into errors just trying to get the tests to run at all. I ended up reaching out to Elizabeth S. for help, and it was valuable. Talking through the code out loud helped me spot things I'd been staring past on my own. Once everything was running, the process of writing tests was pretty satisfying. The bugs that were hardest to catch were the subtle logic ones.
My advice for future students: talk to the professor, tutors, students, write your tests before you fully trust any method, even ones that look simple. And if you're struggling with setup, don't sit alone with it for too long.
